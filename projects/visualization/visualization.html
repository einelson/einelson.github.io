Data visualizations

These are all of my data visualizations! I love trying new visualizations and these are some that I have worked on.

Event/ Causation Graph

Causation is not correlation. This is the first thing I learned when diving into data. Just because your data rises and falls at the same time does not mean that one causes the other to change. I wanted to build a time series-event based graph to help determine what causes change.

This graph is designed with a time-series graph running along the bottom x-axis. Along the top x-axis runs a timeline which matches the time data value of the bottom x-axis.

While you could say that these graphs correlate, when you see the events surrounding both companies you see that different events are causing the change in value.

Screen Time

This visualization was to help me understand how I spend time on my phone. Data was gathered over a 1 week period by day to see which day of the week I spend on my phone the most. I wanted to mostly see how much time I spent on social media so I can cut back from that.

Gold vs Bitcoin

This was a visualization to compare the highs and lows of gold and bitcoin. All values were normalized between 0 and 1 for this.

Set comparison

I was working on a project in python that involved comparing sets of documents pulled from a mongo db. After comparing many sets I realized that the comparison was taking longer and longer. I decided to gather data and make a graph to visualize the comparison time. From the graph you can see in the best case scenario the comparison time is O(n). I assume the outliers were caused by pythons GIL or possibly out of order set.
